Your proof stories
4
Tell me about a fleet-management system you've built. ★ lead with this
OPENThis is the closest thing I've done to your world.
HIT THESE
  • Started building it in-house, then pivoted to building on a third-party carrier's off-the-shelf platform from my own requirements — the integrator move.
  • That became the app + TMS/DMS that scaled the fleet from a small pilot to ~1,800 vans in a year.

  • So I worked with the carrier to automate it: standardized comms, killed manual issue-tracking, alert only on true exceptions, pushed alerts to sub-contractors to self-manage.
  • Result: broke the headcount-grows-with-fleet curve. Maps 1:1 to SBR Connect + the integrator model + exception-based fleet ops.

How do you think about fleet uptime / reliability at scale?
OPENMy rule: the team should know a robot is failing before the customer does.
HIT THESE
  • At Walmart, exception alerting on a performance framework lifted on-time 15% and cut cancellations 35%.
  • For robots, the metrics: uptime, MTBF, delivery success rate, cost-per-delivery.
  • A degraded robot should pull itself out of rotation instead of failing a delivery at the door.
  • Use SBR Connect as the layer that surfaces the true exceptions.
  • Reliability is a metrics-and-process game, not heroics.

Build your own cheat card (steal this)

One structure anyone can copy. The idea: don't memorize answers, prep anchors you can talk from.

1
Write a 60-second opening — your background as a story with a through-line, ending on why this company. It's the answer to "tell me about yourself," and you'll reuse pieces of it all interview.
2
Pick 3 power stories that fit this job, one real number each. Pin them on top and bend most answers back to one.
3
Find their one line — read the company's own words, pull a phrase that's also true of you, and mirror it back.
4
Per question, write an OPEN line + 4-6 "HIT THESE" points + the number — not a script. You read the points and talk like a person.
5
STAR, but short: Situation → Task → Action (the bulk) → Result (the number). Then say it out loud once.
