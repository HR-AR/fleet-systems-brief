Build your own cheat card (steal this)

This card was made with one simple structure. Anyone prepping for a conversational interview can copy it. The whole idea: don't memorize answers, prep anchors you can talk from.

1. Pick your 3 power stories. The ones that fit this exact job. Pin them at the top, lead with them, and bend most answers back to one of them. One real number per story.
2. Find their one line. Read the company's own words (a press release, their site, the JD) and pull one phrase that's also true of you. Mirror it back, it shows you did the work and you fit.
3. For each likely question, write 3 things, not a script: a one-to-two-sentence SAY line, three HIT keyword anchors, and the number to land. You read the anchors and talk like a person.
4. Use STAR, but short: Situation (1 line) → Task → Action (the bulk) → Result (the number). Most people forget the Result, the number is the whole point.
5. Say it out loud once. A mock, a voice memo, a friend. Reading ≠ saying. Tighten anything over 60 seconds.
